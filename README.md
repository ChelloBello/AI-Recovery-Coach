# AI Recovery Coach

Final project for the Building AI course

## Summary

AI Recovery Coach is an AI-powered companion designed to help people recover from gambling addiction. Instead of simply blocking gambling websites, it recognizes emotional and behavioral patterns associated with gambling urges and provides personalized support before a relapse occurs.

---

## Background

Gambling addiction affects millions of people worldwide and often harms not only the gambler but also their family and friends.

Growing up, I witnessed how gambling addiction affected not only gamblers but also their families and loved ones. This inspired me to think about a different approach. Most existing applications focus on blocking gambling websites or limiting access to casinos. While these tools are useful, they often intervene too late.

The real problem usually starts long before someone opens a gambling website. Common triggers include:

* Stress
* Loneliness
* Boredom
* Anxiety
* Payday
* Relationship problems

Instead of simply blocking gambling, my idea is to recognize these emotional patterns early and provide support before the user makes the decision to gamble.

---

## How is it used?

The application acts as a personal AI recovery companion.

Users voluntarily provide information such as:

* Daily mood
* Stress level
* Sleep quality
* Gambling urges
* Journal entries
* Personal notes

Using this information, the AI learns the user's personal behavioral patterns and identifies situations that may indicate a high risk of relapse.

Instead of saying:

> "Don't gamble."

the AI could say:

> "You've reported feeling stressed for several days. Would you like to talk for five minutes before making any decisions today?"

The goal is not to control the user but to encourage reflection and healthier decisions.

Potential future features include:

* Daily AI conversations
* Personalized recovery plans
* Progress tracking
* Motivational reminders
* Crisis intervention mode

## Data sources and AI methods

### Possible data sources

* Daily mood tracking
* User journal entries
* Sleep information
* Gambling history
* Voluntarily shared smartwatch data
* Anonymous recovery statistics

### AI methods

* Natural Language Processing (NLP)
* Sentiment Analysis
* Machine Learning for relapse prediction
* Pattern Recognition
* Recommendation Systems

The AI would continuously improve its recommendations based on each user's recovery journey.

---

## Challenges

This application cannot cure gambling addiction on its own.

It should never replace psychologists, therapists, or professional addiction treatment.

The AI may sometimes make incorrect predictions, and users must remain free to make their own decisions.

Because the application processes sensitive emotional information, protecting user privacy and securely storing personal data would be essential.

False positives and false negatives are inevitable, therefore the AI should always support rather than make decisions for the user.

---

## What next?

Future versions could include:

* Smartwatch integration for stress detection
* Voice conversations with the AI
* Anonymous peer-support communities
* Therapist dashboard (with user permission)
* Financial budgeting tools
* Personalized long-term recovery plans

The long-term vision is to build an AI companion that not only helps prevent relapse, but also supports people in rebuilding healthier habits, stronger relationships, and a better quality of life. The goal is not simply to stop gambling, but to help people rediscover hope during some of the most difficult moments of recovery.
